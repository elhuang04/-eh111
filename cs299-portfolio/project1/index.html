Honesty

Situation: Alice enters the White Rabbit's house and drinks from a bottle labeled "DRINK ME," causing her to grow uncontrollably large. She soon realizes her mistake and admits to it honestly.

Virtue Connection: Alice's honesty about her mistake highlights the importance of being truthful, even in difficult situations. According to the virtue of honesty, one must always strive to be truthful and transparent. This honesty helps her learn and grow from her experiences, emphasizing that acknowledging our errors is crucial for personal development.

Empathy

Situation: During her encounter with the Mock Turtle and the Gryphon, Alice listens empathetically to the Mock Turtle's sorrowful story about his life and education.

Virtue Connection: Alice's willingness to listen and understand the Mock Turtle's feelings demonstrates her empathy. Empathy involves recognizing and valuing the emotions of others. Alice's attentive listening and compassionate response show the importance of being sensitive to the experiences and emotions of others, fostering a more caring and understanding world.

Courage

Situation: In the chaotic environment of the Queen's croquet game, Alice remains composed and continues to play, despite the threat of the Queen's temper and the absurd nature of the game.

Virtue Connection: Alice's courage is evident as she faces potential danger with bravery and determination. Courage involves facing fears and challenges with resolve. By staying calm and composed in the face of the Queen's unpredictable temper, Alice exemplifies how courage enables one to handle difficult situations with strength and grace.
